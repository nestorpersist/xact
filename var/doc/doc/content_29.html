In @x:Act, it is necessary to define the value that results from a sequence of expressions or commands. The rules for this are called join rules.

  • When a sequence of expression is empty then the result is the value null.
  • When a sequence of expressions has exactly one expression then the value is the value of that expression.
  • When an sequence of expressions has two expressions, and both have type string, the result is the concatenation of those strings (see ++).
  • When a sequence of expressions has two expressions and one expression has value null, then the result is the value of the other expression.
  • When a sequence of expressions has three of more expressions, then the first two expressions are joined producing a sequence that is one element shorter. That resulting shorter sequence is then joined.

Join rules are also used for @x:while and @x:for loops. Here the result will be the join of all the body executions.
